Who Are We?

The Australian Asbestos Stories website has been created by a team of media researchers at Murdoch University in Perth, Western Australia to give you easy access to information on asbestos-related diseases, health information for work and home, and to tell the history of asbestos in Australia and abroad. The project aims to develop an online resource everything Asbestos; its historical legacy, current impacts on Australian society and the ways in which we can all help to minimise its harmful effects on our health and wellbeing.

Asbestos Stories

Asbestos Stories brings together the many and varied stories and experiences of people in the community who have had links with asbestos.

Many of the stories relate to shared experiences in minesites and workplaces. Many deal with the trauma resulting from direct experience with illnesses – the deadly legacy of asbestos. The important thing is that these are personal stories and we thank all the contributors for sharing them with us.

History of Asbestos

In this section we explore the history of asbestos, from the earliest years of mining the mineral, through to its manufacture and use as Australia’s main building material in the postwar years.

We also look at its health legacy and recount the long and ongoing legal battles as workers have sought compensation from companies who kept them in the dark about the grave risks from asbestos exposure.

Learn About Asbestos

We focus on the medical information relating to asbestos-related diseases

In this section we focus on the medical information relating to asbestos-related diseases. The information provided is designed to complement, not replace, the relationship between a patient and his/her own physician.

We introduce you to the Australian researchers and their research projects as they seek to understand the causes of asbestos-related diseases and to find possible cures.
